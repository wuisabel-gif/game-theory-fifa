The tactical battlefield

Three tactics, no winner among them — they chase each other in a cycle. That cycle is exactly why the engine must mix.

Hover a cell: each is Side A's expected goal difference for that tactic matchup. Green favours A, red favours B.

Attack beats Balanced, Balanced beats Park the bus, Park the bus beats Attack. Because the advantage runs in a circle, there's no single best tactic — the stable answer is a probability over all three. That's the Nash equilibrium.

How the engine works

Each team's Elo rating sets baseline expected goals. On top, both managers play a 3-tactic game with a rock-paper-scissors structure, solved for a Nash equilibrium by fictitious play (the "Watch Nash Emerge" tab shows this live). The equilibrium expected goals feed a Poisson scoreline model with a Dixon–Coles correction for realistic draws. Campaign matches sample real scorelines and settle ties on penalties. Validated against the 2022 World Cup — see the accuracy workbook and README.

Elo ratings are illustrative estimates, not a live feed; this is a toy for exploring game theory, not betting advice.

Roadmap (next): a 5-formation strategy layer (turning the 3×3 game into 5×5), a Historical Upset explorer, and a match-replay commentary generator. The engine is structured so these drop in on top of the same core.
